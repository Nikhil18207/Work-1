## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This policy establishes the framework for all food procurement activities across our organization. It applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties involved in sourcing, purchasing, and managing food and beverage products.

### 1.1 Objectives
- Ensure food safety and quality compliance
- Optimize procurement costs while maintaining quality standards
- Promote sustainable and ethical sourcing practices
- Manage supply chain risks effectively
- Maintain transparency and traceability

---

## 2. SUPPLIER SELECTION CRITERIA

### 2.1 Mandatory Requirements
All suppliers MUST meet the following minimum criteria:

#### Food Safety Certifications
- **ISO 22000** or equivalent food safety management system
- **HACCP** certification for all food processing facilities
- **Local regulatory compliance** (FDA, EFSA, FSSAI, etc.)

#### Quality Standards
- Minimum quality rating: 4.0/5.0
- Delivery reliability: Minimum 90%
- Product consistency: < 2% defect rate

#### Financial Stability
- Minimum 3 years in business
- Financial health score: B+ or higher
- Product liability insurance: Minimum USD 2M coverage

### 2.2 Preferred Criteria
Suppliers meeting these criteria receive priority consideration:

- **Sustainability certifications:** Organic, Fair Trade, Rainforest Alliance, RSPO
- **Quality excellence:** ISO 9001, FSSC 22000, BRC, SQF
- **Ethical sourcing:** SA8000, Fair Labor practices
- **Innovation capability:** R&D facilities, new product development
- **Digital capabilities:** EDI integration, real-time tracking, blockchain traceability

---

## 3. PRODUCT CATEGORY REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 Vegetable Oils & Fats

#### Quality Requirements
- Peroxide value: < 10 meq/kg (standard), < 5 meq/kg (premium)
- Free fatty acids: < 0.1% (refined), < 0.5% (cold-pressed)
- Moisture content: < 0.1%
- No trans fats (< 0.5g per serving)

#### Sustainability Requirements
- **Palm oil:** RSPO certified mandatory
- **Organic oils:** EU Organic or USDA Organic certification
- **Traceability:** Farm-to-factory traceability required
- **Deforestation-free:** Zero deforestation commitment

#### Sourcing Preferences
1. **Tier 1:** Organic, sustainably sourced, local (< 500 km)
2. **Tier 2:** Certified sustainable, regional (< 2000 km)
3. **Tier 3:** Conventional, international (with certifications)

### 3.2 Grains & Flours

#### Quality Requirements
- Protein content: As per product specification
- Moisture: < 14% for storage stability
- Mycotoxin levels: Below regulatory limits
- Pesticide residues: Below MRL (Maximum Residue Limits)

#### Sourcing Requirements
- **Non-GMO preferred** for consumer-facing products
- **Organic certification** for premium product lines
- **Allergen control:** Dedicated production lines for allergen-free products

### 3.3 Spices & Seasonings

#### Quality Requirements
- Microbial limits: As per ASTA (American Spice Trade Association) standards
- Volatile oil content: Minimum as per specification
- No artificial colors or additives
- Irradiation disclosure required

#### Sourcing Requirements
- **Fair Trade certification** preferred
- **Direct farm sourcing** for premium spices
- **Traceability:** Origin verification mandatory

## 5. RISK MANAGEMENT

### 5.1 Supply Chain Risk Assessment

#### High-Risk Categories (Require Dual Sourcing)
- Single-origin products (e.g., specific regional oils)
- Products with high price volatility (e.g., palm oil, soy)
- Suppliers in geopolitically unstable regions
- Products with limited supplier base

#### Risk Mitigation Strategies
- **Dual sourcing:** Minimum 2 suppliers for critical categories
- **Safety stock:** 30-60 days for high-risk products
- **Long-term contracts:** 12-24 months for price stability
- **Hedging:** Financial hedging for volatile commodities

### 5.2 Quality Risk Management
- **Pre-shipment inspection:** Mandatory for new suppliers
- **Regular audits:** Annual supplier audits for critical suppliers
- **Quality escrow:** Hold 5% of payment pending quality confirmation
- **Continuous monitoring:** Real-time quality dashboards

### 5.3 Compliance Risk
- **Regulatory monitoring:** Track changing food safety regulations
- **Certification verification:** Annual verification of all certifications
- **Audit trails:** Maintain complete documentation for 7 years
- **Incident response:** 24-hour response protocol for food safety incidents

## 6. SUSTAINABILITY AND ETHICAL SOURCING

### 6.1 Environmental Commitments
- **Carbon footprint:** Reduce supply chain emissions by 30% by 2030
- **Sustainable packaging:** 100% recyclable or biodegradable by 2026
- **Water stewardship:** Prefer suppliers with water conservation programs
- **Zero waste:** Target zero waste to landfill by 2028

### 6.2 Social Responsibility
- **No child labor:** Zero tolerance policy
- **Fair wages:** Living wage compliance required
- **Worker safety:** ISO 45001 or equivalent preferred
- **Community impact:** Support local communities and smallholder farmers

### 6.3 Animal Welfare (if applicable)
- **Humane treatment:** Compliance with animal welfare standards
- **Certifications:** Certified Humane, Global Animal Partnership

---

## 7. PAYMENT TERMS AND CONDITIONS

### 7.1 Standard Payment Terms
- **Net 30:** Standard for established suppliers
- **Net 45-60:** For suppliers offering competitive pricing
- **Early payment discounts:** 2/10 Net 30 (2% discount if paid within 10 days)

### 7.2 Payment Security
- **Letter of Credit:** Required for new international suppliers
- **Bank guarantees:** For large orders (> USD 100,000)
- **Escrow payments:** For quality-critical products

---

## 8. CONTRACT MANAGEMENT

### 8.1 Contract Types
- **Spot purchases:** For one-time or opportunistic buys
- **Short-term contracts:** 3-6 months for testing new suppliers
- **Annual contracts:** 12 months for established suppliers
- **Multi-year contracts:** 24-36 months for strategic partnerships

### 8.2 Contract Review
- **Quarterly reviews:** Pricing, quality, delivery performance
- **Annual renegotiation:** Terms, volumes, sustainability targets
- **Performance scorecards:** Monthly KPI tracking

## 10. EXCEPTIONS AND WAIVERS

### 10.1 Emergency Procurement
In case of supply disruptions or emergencies:
- Expedited approval process (within 24 hours)
- Temporary waiver of non-critical requirements
- Post-event review and documentation mandatory

### 10.2 Single-Source Justification
Single-source procurement allowed only if:
- Unique product with no alternatives
- Proprietary technology or formulation
- Emergency situation
- Requires VP approval and documented justification

## 11. COMPLIANCE AND MONITORING

### 11.1 Policy Compliance
- **Training:** Annual procurement policy training mandatory
- **Audits:** Internal audits twice per year
- **Violations:** Disciplinary action as per HR policy

### 11.2 Performance Metrics
- **Cost savings:** Target 5-10% annual savings
- **Supplier quality:** > 95% on-time, in-full delivery
- **Sustainability:** 80% spend with certified sustainable suppliers by 2026
- **Risk management:** Zero critical supply disruptions
